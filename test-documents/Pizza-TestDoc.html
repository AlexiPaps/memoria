The Perfect Pizza Margherita

The queen of all pizzas. Simple. Sacred. Protected by law in Italy since 2009.

Ingredients (for 1 pizza)

  • 250g pizza dough (preferably 00 flour, 65% hydration, 48–72h cold ferment)
  • 80–100g San Marzano tomatoes (crushed by hand)
  • 100–120g fresh mozzarella di bufala or fior di latte
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • Extra virgin olive oil
  • Fine sea salt

The Rules (Official AVPN Specification)

  1. Oven temperature: 430–480°C (800–900°F)
  2. Baking time: 60–90 seconds
  3. Dough must be hand-kneaded or low-speed mixer — no rolling pin!
  4. Tomatoes: only San Marzano DOP or Pomodorino del Piennolo del Vesuvio
  5. Cheese: torn by hand, never sliced
  6. Basil added after baking (never cooked)

Step-by-Step

1. Preheat your oven (and pizza stone/steel) to maximum — ideally 500°C+.
2. Stretch dough by hand — never roll.
3. Spread crushed tomatoes in a thin layer, leaving 2cm border.
4. Drizzle with olive oil + pinch of salt.
5. Bake 60–90 seconds until leopard-spotted.
6. Remove → tear mozzarella → scatter → fresh basil → final drizzle of oil.
Pro tip: The center should be soft and slightly soupy ("a piscina" — a pool). If it's dry, it's not a real Neapolitan pizza.

Common Mistakes

  • Too much cheese → soggy center
  • Too much sauce → raw dough
  • Using low-quality mozzarella → rubbery texture
  • Overloading toppings → this is not American pizza!
